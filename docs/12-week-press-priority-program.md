# PART II — THE FINAL PROGRAM

## 1. Guiding Principles

The goal hierarchy left fingerprints on every structural decision. The presses got the wave because waved 5s/3s/2s loading is the highest-yield tool in the Pavel/Prilepin toolbox — frequent, submaximal, varied-but-frozen skill practice — and the two lifts you want to move most deserve the best tool and the freshest slots (Monday and Thursday open their sessions; nothing precedes a press except its primer). The second weekly exposure at a fixed 70% is pure Pavel greasing-the-groove: unchanging, crisp, never a cost. The squat is treated with Dan John pragmatism — real volume, an 80% ceiling, and progression by sets so it improves without ever billing the recovery account the presses draw on. The deadlift is run as Easy Strength: 13 dead-stop reps a week, never the hardest thing in any week, because a 515 puller maintains with astonishingly little and every extra deadlift rep is paid for in press recovery and tennis legs. Boyle governs the accessory layer (unilateral daily, weight room serves the sport), McGill governs the trunk (anti-movement only, rotation budgeted against the serve), Verkhoshansky keeps the athletic layer tiny and sharp, and Hatfield/Tuchscherer govern every rep: maximal intent, submaximal load, bar speed as the referee.

## 3. Global Rules

1. **The wave.** Each 4-week block runs 5s → 3s → 2s → deload on both presses; loads step up between blocks. Double-week set counts taper as intensity rises across blocks: 4×2 (86%) → 3×2 (88%) → 2×2 (~91%/90%).
2. **RIR ≥2 on every set of everything.** No failure, grinders, AMRAPs, drop sets, or 1RM testing before the Week-12 taper.
3. **Bar speed is the stop signal.** Two consecutive slowed reps end the set — mid-set, no negotiation. The sheet proposes; the bar disposes.
4. **CAT.** Every concentric, every load, driven at maximal intended velocity.
5. **Grindy-double rule.** Any heavy double that grinds → next set is automatically −5 lb. Logged, not debated.
6. **Second press slots (v1.3 — both now non-barbell).** Monday: standing 1-arm KB press 3×5/side, RIR 3, start 20–24 kg. Thursday: incline DB press 3×8, RIR 2–3. Both progress on the §9.5 trigger. Stated plainly: bench and OHP are each barbell-practiced once per week now — the wave carries the entire skill dose, and both swaps carry revert triggers tied to the Week-13 tests (Coach's Note 9).
7. **Deadlift discipline.** Dead-stop every rep. Triples and the 2×2 only. No touch-and-go. No singles, ever — including "just to feel it" in Week 10–11.
8. **Squat ceiling: 225 lb (80%), all 12 weeks.** Progression is added sets across blocks (3 → 4 → 5), never added load above the cap.
9. **Rests.** Main barbell 3:00 · practice press 2:00 · weighted calisthenics 2:00 or supersetted into press rests · accessories hard-capped 75 s · carries/core 45–60 s.
10. **Cut order when the 60-minute working clock threatens** (warm-up is off the clock): accessories trim first — on upper days incline DB, then rows, then dips (dips protected by athlete preference, v1.2); on lower days rotational toss first → second-slot press trims next (Mon KB press / Thu incline DB) → main lift never below 4 working sets (Tue: never below 2×2 + 2×3) → **the primer is never cut.**
11. **Pattern distribution.** Unilateral work every day; quad-biased unilateral on deadlift day, hinge-biased on squat day — never stack the day's main chain. A loaded carry every session. One dedicated anti-movement core slot daily; all four categories weekly; zero spinal flexion anywhere.
12. **Plyo dose is frozen at 3×3/day**, first in the session, full reset per rep. Variations advance between blocks; volume never does.
13. **Deloads are obeyed, not audited.** Weeks 4 and 8: main lifts ~70%, all accessories 2 sets, everything RIR 3–4.

## 9. Autoregulation & Advancement Rules

1. **Slow-rep rule (global):** two consecutive slowed reps end the set immediately, at any load, on any exercise. Bar speed outranks the spreadsheet.
2. **Grindy-double rule:** any Week-3-type double that grinds → next set automatically −5 lb. If the reduced set also grinds, the day's main work is over; move on without ceremony.
3. **Sub-2-RIR handling:** if a prescribed set arrives at <2 RIR, drop 5 lb and finish the prescription. Twice in one session → end main-lift work for the day. **Twice in one week → next week runs the deload template**, wherever it falls on the calendar.
4. **Tennis-wrecked-legs protocol (Friday morning check):** if legs rate >7/10 heavy after Wednesday tennis + Thursday lifting, Friday squat drops one week back in the load wave, −1 set, and swings cut to 2×10. Log it; don't moralize it.
5. **Calisthenics progression trigger:** when every set of a movement holds RIR 3+ across a full session, add +2.5–5 lb the following week (Block 2 is the natural window). Never chase a rep PR.
6. **Court quality is the canary:** tennis sharpness is the leading indicator of systemic recovery. Two consecutive flat, heavy-legged, or sloppy sessions → the following training week becomes the deload template, no debate. The weight room exists to serve the athlete; a plan that dulls the sport is failing regardless of what the bar says.
7. **No singles, ever, restated where it matters:** the Week 10–11 urge to pull "one easy 465 just to feel it" spends recovery the presses have already claimed. The deadlift's test window is after the press tests, optional, and no sooner.
8. **Deloads are load-bearing:** weeks 4 and 8 are part of the progression, not a pause in it. Showing up fresh in weeks 5 and 9 *is* the adaptation.
9. **Protected weeks — 3, 7, 10, 11 (v1.2).** These are the four crack points (heavy doubles/triples colliding with peak accumulated sport load). Standing rules for each: (a) all accessories drop to their low end — 2 sets, RIR 3–4, no calisthenics load PRs; (b) no hard tennis before the Thursday OHP session; (c) the DL heavy 2×2 runs only if the first rep's bar speed is excellent, otherwise it converts to a third triple at the volume load; (d) Sunday of the prior weekend is a strict flat Z2 ride, non-negotiable.
10. **Directional interference coupling (v1.2).** Acute sport fatigue pre-cuts the *next* session, in a fixed order that protects the lifts you value: heavy serving the day before an upper day trims that day's second-slot press first (Mon KB press / Thu incline DB), then gorilla/1-arm row — **not dips** (dips move only on a genuine shoulder signal or last in the time cut order). A long or hilly ride the day before a lower day trims that day's snatch/swing volume and unilateral work first, main lift untouched.

## 10. Sport Integration

The real week: **Mon lift · Tue lift · Wed tennis · Thu lift · Fri lift · Sat tennis · Sun ride.** Say it plainly: **this week contains zero true rest days.** That is a deliberate, eyes-open trade — and it survives only under behavioral rules, not good intentions:

1. **Sunday is Zone 2 or it's a liability.** Conversational pace, ≤90 minutes, no group-ride ego surges. Cycling is the plan's entire conditioning budget *and* its only pseudo-recovery day; if it becomes a workout, the week has eight stressors and a deficit.
2. **Sleep is a programmed session:** ≥7.5 h floor. It's the only genuine recovery input the schedule permits.
3. **The Friday→Saturday turnaround is the tightest seam.** The 80% squat cap, the Week-11 5×2, and the frozen swing dose all exist to hand Saturday tennis fresh legs. If a squat session ever visibly taxes Saturday, invoke Rule 9.4 the following week preemptively.
4. **Wednesday tennis is a stressor, not recovery** — it's sprint and rotational work. The Thursday OHP day is built light on legs (nothing beyond carries and the gorilla row's static hinge) partly for this reason.
5. **Extra/social tennis replaces the Sunday ride, never a lift** — and if it lands Thursday night, Friday runs the wrecked-legs protocol by default.
6. **Rotation is budgeted:** the gym adds only 4 light rotational sets/week because the court already supplies hundreds of high-velocity rotations. More gym rotation is not more tennis power; it's more elbow and oblique mileage.
7. **Directional interference (see §9.10):** heavy serving pre-cuts the next upper day's second-slot press and rows before anything else — dips are held, per your preference, and move only on a real shoulder signal. Long/hilly rides pre-cut the next lower day's ballistic and unilateral volume, never the main lift.

## 11. Known Trade-offs / Coach's Notes

1. **Deadlift is deliberately under-dosed vs. Prilepin** (13 reps/wk; the 2×2 sits at 4 reps against a 10-rep band minimum). Governing mechanism: Easy Strength maintenance per goal #3. Expect the deadlift to hold or drift up slightly, not jump. That is the assignment.
2. **Heavy-double weeks under-fill the 80s band** (8 → 6 → 4 press reps vs. a 10-rep band floor). Governing mechanism: intensity-skill exposure with mandated set taper and RIR ≥2; the weekly 70% practice slot backfills quality reps. Hypertrophy stimulus is knowingly foregone in those weeks.
3. **Squat weeks 3 and 11 sit 2 reps under the 70s band minimum** (9–10 reps). Governing mechanism: tennis freshness (goal #4) and test-week preservation. Accepted.
4. **Week-3-type double intensity (86–91%) at RIR ≥2 is a knife's edge for the bench** — a 91% double is honestly RIR ~2 on a good day and RIR 1.5 on a bad one. The 5-lb drop rule and the crispness mandate are the guardrails; use them without ego.
5. **Pooled pressing-family volume (20 sets/wk) exceeds the 10–15 surplus when bench, OHP, and dips are summed.** Per muscle group it's compliant (chest 13, delts 7), but the shoulder is the plan's #1 overuse surface given two waves + dips + plyo push-ups + snatches + serves. **This grew by athlete request** (full dips restored, snatches added) — an eyes-open call that the coach supports but flags: the mitigations are now genuinely load-bearing, not decorative. Order of defense on any shoulder signal: snatches revert to swings → incline DB trims → dips drop → practice OHP drops. Dips are late in that queue by your preference, but they are *in* it — "don't reduce dips" is a programming default, not a pain-override.
6. **Lumbar load is concentrated on Tuesday** (heavy pulls + swings + rotation in one session). Mitigations: dead-stop resets, McGill-clean rotation dose, Pallof placed last when the spine is warmest, and 48+ hours before the next hinge exposure (Friday's SL-RDL is light).
7. **Concurrent-training interference is real and unbudgeted-for by any program on paper:** four lifts + two tennis + one ride with no rest day means this plan runs closer to its recovery ceiling than its volume numbers suggest. The canary rule (9.6) and two-strike deload trigger (9.3) are the honest admission that autoregulation, not the calendar, is the real periodization.
8. **No direct calf or arm isolation.** Chins, dips, rows, jumps, carries, and tennis cover them at maintenance-plus. Adding isolation would bill the 60-minute cap for returns the goal hierarchy never asked for.
9. **(v1.1/v1.3) Both second barbell press exposures are gone, by athlete request.** Thursday's paused bench became incline DB press (v1.1); Monday's OHP practice became standing 1-arm KB press (v1.3). Pressing *volume* is fully preserved and arguably more shoulder-friendly, but bench-the-skill and OHP-the-skill are each now practiced once a week — the wave carries the entire barbell skill dose, which is the largest single deviation from the original press-priority architecture. Verdict: an eyes-open bet that volume substitutes for barbell frequency at this training age. Revert triggers, checked at the Week-13 tests: bench single under ~222 → reinstate Thursday touch-and-go 3×5 @ 150 next cycle; OHP single under ~130 → reinstate Monday barbell OHP 3×5 @ 90 next cycle. Whichever lift underperforms gets its barbell frequency back first.
10. **(v1.1) Snatches spend from the shoulder budget.** An overhead ballistic now joins two press waves, dips, plyo push-ups, and serves. They replaced Tuesday swings one-for-one (zero net volume added; Friday keeps pure swings), and they sit *first* in the shoulder-defense queue (v1.2): any shoulder complaint reverts snatches to swings that same session, before incline DB or dips move at all.
